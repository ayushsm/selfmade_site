Disclaimer: Since I ended up making this website in June 2019, it seemed kind of hard to keep a calendar-like tracking system of my thoughts, life, or whatever else I put on the blog due to, well, the past being kind of a long time ago and all that. However, I also wanted to use the timline to experiment with my website (and hopefully it'll continue into the future), so I wanted to include my first couple of college years, which were really important to me. As a result, I decided to dedicate the 2017 and 2018 pages to being more of a summary of those years.

Originally I wrote this entire post without talking about the first half of my freshman year or any of my high school career but after writing a lot of it (and in retrospect), I think a lot of that time changed the person I would eventually become, so a lot of this post include my time in 2016. I think a large reason that that's the case is because I think I genuinely enjoyed high school to the point that I enjoy reminiscing about it. A lot of the friends and people I talk to in college have expressed that their high school, in some way or another, was not that enjoyable, especially in comparison to their experiences in college.

In that first semester of college though, the only thing I wished for was to go back to something as simple as my senior year in high school. A lot of my expectations for college, for better or for worse, were based off what a lot people see in movies and TV shows.
